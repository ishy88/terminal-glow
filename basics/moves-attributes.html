Attributes

There are five attributes in Terminal Glow that the player characters use to make moves:

  • Cool - Your ability to remain calm and focused in stressful situations.
  • Edge - Your street-savvy and ability to give the impression that you are a badass.
  • Meat - Your physical talents without the aid of cyberware.
  • Mind - Your mental abilities: logic, instict, reasoning, creativity, or any other mental process.
  • Style - Your charisma, presence, or nerve.

The rating for each attribute is a bonus or penalty, ranging from -1 to +3.

Moves

The most basic unit of rules in Terminal Glow is a move. A move is a passage of text triggered when your character takes a discreet action within the fiction that specifies which attribute to roll and assigns specific benefits or drawbacks to the die results. As stated in the basic rules overview, a 10+ will always be a full success, a 7-9 will be a bonus with some drawback, and a 6- will either be an outright failure or a success at a steep cost.

Basic Moves

The following moves are considered basic moves. They are moves that any character can trigger, regardless of playbook.

Act Under Pressure (Cool)

When you race against the clock, act while in danger or act to avoid danger, roll +Cool:

 10+: You do it, no problem.

 7-9: You stumble, hesitate, or flinch: the GM will offer you a worse outcome, hard bargain, or ugly choice.

Assess (Edge)

When you closely study a person, place or situation, or when you quickly size up an opponent or a charged situation, roll +Edge:

 10+: Gain 3 hold.

 7-9: Gain 1 hold.

In the ensuing action, you may spend 1 hold at any time to ask the GM a question from the list below if your examination could have revealed the answer. The GM may ask you questions to clarify your intent. Gain a +1 bonus to your next roll while acting on the answer to each question.

  • What potential complication do I need to be wary of?
  • What do I notice despite an effort to conceal it?
  • How is ______ vulnerable to me?
  • How can I avoid trouble or hide here?
  • What is my best way in/out/past?
  • Where can I gain the most advantage?
  • Who or what is my biggest threat in this situation?
  • Who or what is in control here?

Declare a Contact (None)

When you need to call in a favour from a new contact, name and describe the Contact, then say why the Contact owes you a favour or why you owe them a favour. The GM will ask you some questions about the Contact and your relationship. Add the Contact to your list (no roll required!).

You can only declare a contact once per mission, although certain playbook moves may allow you to gain more contacts in similar ways.

Fast Talk (Style)

When you try to convince someone to do what you want with promises, lies or bluster, roll +Style:

 10+: NPCs do what you want. PCs choose whether to do it or not. If they do, they mark experience. If they don’t, they must act under pressure to go against your stated wishes.

 7-9: NPCs do it, but someone will find out: Your crew takes 1 heat. For PCs, choose whether they mark experience if they do what you want or if they must act under pressure to go against your stated wishes. Then it's up to them.

Hack the Planet (Mind)

Special: While it's possible to hack the planet without the aid of a neural interface and a cyberdeck, a character who lacks these objects will not be able to fully interface with the machine. Hacking attempts that do not use both of these items take -2 and cannot select to suffer harm on a 9-.

When you attempt to hack a system with a specific objective in mind, state that objective and roll +Mind:

 10+: You accomplish your goal, no strings attached.

 7-9: You’re in, but choose one:

  • Your physical location is logged.
  • Your identity is compromised.
  • You suffer 1 harm from lethal feedback algorithms.
  • An alarm is triggered.

 6-: You’re locked out and the GM chooses two.

Hit the Street (Style)

When you go to a Contact for help, roll +Style:

 10+: You get what you want, for the standard price.

 7-9: You get what you want, but choose 2 from the list below:

  • Your request is going to cost you extra cash or resources.
  • Your request is going to take some time to put together.
  • Your request is going to attract unwanted attention, complications, or consequences.
  • Your contact needs you to help them out with something. If you turn them down take -1 ongoing to this move till you make it right.

Mix it Up (Meat)

When you use violence against an armed force to seize control of an objective, state that objective and roll +Meat:

 10+: You take control of your objective.

 7-9: You take control of your objective, but choose two:

  • You make too much noise. Your crew takes 1 heat.
  • You suffer harm appropriate to the situation.
  • An ally suffers harm appropriate to the situation.
  • Something of value is broken.

Play Hardball (Edge)

When you get in someone's face threatening violence and you intend to carry through, roll +Edge:

 10+: NPCs will do what you want. PCs can either do what you want or suffer an appropriate consequence.

 7-9: For NPCs, the GM chooses 1:

  • They attempt to remove you as a threat, but not before suffering appropriate consequences.
  • They do it, but they want payback. Lose 1 status with a relevant faction.
  • They do it, but they tell someone about it. Your crew takes 1 heat.

PCs again choose to either do what you want or suffer an appropriate consequence, but they gain a +1 bonus to their next roll to act against you.

Research (Mind)

When you investigate a person, place, object, or service using a library, dossier or database (or combination of them), ask a question from the list below and roll +Mind:

  • Where would I find ______?
  • How secure is ______?
  • Who or what is related to ______?
  • Who owned or employed ______?
  • Who or what is ______ most valuable to?
  • What is the relationship between ______ and ______?

 10+: The GM will answer your question & answer a follow-up question from the list as well.

 7-9: The GM will answer your question.

 6-: The GM will answer your question and decide on a consequence.

Resist (Variable)

When you attempt to resist a consequence inflicted on your character the GM chooses to either downgrade the consequence or clear it entirely, then roll a number of dice equal to 1 + your rating in the most relevant attribute chosen from Cool, Meat, and Style. Suffer stress equal to 6 - the highest value of the die rolled. If you roll two sixes, additionally clear one stress.

If the number of dice you roll to resist would be reduced below 1, instead roll two dice and suffer stress equal to 6 - the lowest value of the die rolled. Two sixes rolled in this way do not clear any stress.

Teamwork Moves

Additional moves that any PC can preform are detailed in Teamwork. These teamwork moves all relate to aiding or otherwise working with the rest of your crew.
